net.art + artware

we're going to be creating our own experimental web based drawing apps. as artists, when exploring new domains, we often start by copying other artists. we'll be drawing inspiration from work at the intersection of two new media art sub-genres: Internet art + software art. the former refers to art that embraces the Internet as a creative medium. like the Web itself, it began in the mid 1990s, the first wave of internet artists is often referred to as the net.art movement. you can find interactive tutorials on this topic on netnet.studio: net.art (part 1) when artists discovered HTML as well as net.art (part 2) from ASCII Art to Form Art.

software art (aka artware) is an umbrella term for all manner of experimental software projects by artists, hackers && other creative technologists that explore software (apps, programs, exectubales, etc) as a creative medium. Where mainstream software is typically aimed at providing some utility for the user, artware departs from this convention to explore a number of other themes && concerns. You can read more about this niche and explore loads of examples in our notes on software art.
